Worbles – the simulation of society building.

by Brad Moore - (c) 2005, all rights reserved

 

 

This simulation is meant as a fun exercise but really bears no resemblance to any real system.  To create challenge I have manipulated the factors that both randomly interfere with your success as well as the factors that control the game element you control.

 

Object:

 

The object of the game is to create worbles who can grow grain to be sold.  You have 50 years (turns) to build your treasury from 0 to 200,000 cash units.  Do not let your worble population drop below 1 as this is a losing condition.

 

Strategy:

 

To succeed you must develop worble technology.  This is done by training scientists.  Each scientist is capable to producing a specific amount of study power (called Study Units).  Beware – scientist training costs money.  Assign your scientists study units to various areas of study.  You can improve Farmer efficiency,  Hatchery worker efficiency, Builder Efficiency, Scientific Efficiency and Marketing ability.

 

Each turn (one year) consists of several actions:

 

1)      Feed your worbles.  Each worble requires 4 units of grain.  If you do not have enough grain, the unfed worbles will die.

2)      Sell Excess grain for a profit.  The selling price varies from year to year.  Save it when the sales price is low and sell more when it is high.  You can increase the selling range by studying marketing.

3)      Assigning worbles to tasks.  There are four things worbles can be doing: Building, Farming, Scientific research and Hatching more worbles.

4)      Divide up and assign any study units to area you wish to research.

5)      Processing the end of year events.

 

Many things happen at the end of the year:

 

1)      Check to see if all worbles are housed.  A single builder can create and maintain one home.  Initially dwelling capacity of the home is four worbles.  Researching Building can increase the dwelling capacity.

2)      Harvest grain from cultivated fields.  Initially farmers can only cultivate a half acre at a time.  Research Farming to increase this.  Each acre yields 20 units of grain.

3)      Create new worbles.  Worbles are hatched in a hatchery.  Each hatchery worker can hatch 0 to 4 worbles initially.  Research Hatchery to increase this amount.

4)      Assess scientific progress.  Applying study units to the required amount of study.  You will observe a progress bar showing how much study has been done and how much is left to do.  Each time the required amount of study is completed, you will advance a technology level in that area.  The required amount of study increases exponentially as you progress technology levels.

5)      Disasters – these are random occurrences that add a degree of chance to you game.  The most common will be a poor harvest which results in a 25% loss of crops.  Dissent will cause 5% of your worble population to leave.  A severe drought will result in a 55% loss of crops usually resulting in a lack of food for all your worbles to eat.  Plague and War round out the bad stuff.  The odds of a bad event occurring increases as the years go on, adding difficulty to the game.

 

User interface:

 

·        Buttons at the top right are used to move through the various phases of play.  They are labeled “Feed”, “Sell” and “Next Year”. 

 

·        Assigning worbles to tasks and worble study units to areas of research is accomplished by pressing small “+” and “-“ keys to increase or decrease the quantity or worbles/study units assigned to the item.  By default they are moved in quantities of one each.  If you want to move groups of worbles/study units you can increase the scale from 1 to 10 or 100 and move that quantity in a single click.  The scale will reset itself to 1 at the end of year processing.

 

·        Pay attention to the bottom of Information box.  Important messages will be displayed in yellow text there.
